DNA language models, genomic AI, and sequence-to-function prediction.

I develop DNA language models and computational genomics tools that connect sequence, regulation, and phenotype. My current focus is on foundation models for long DNA sequences, including GENA-LM and related DNALM systems, while building on earlier work in 3D genomics, chromatin architecture, and variant interpretation.

Research areas

Genome architecture and disease

Vision

My long-term goal is to make genomic AI genuinely useful for biology and biotechnology: models that learn reusable DNA representations, transfer across tasks, and help connect sequence to regulation, expression, and phenotype.

I am particularly interested in building foundation models that can support genome annotation, noncoding variant interpretation, promoter design, and other sequence-to-function applications at scale. In this view, DNALMs are not just benchmark models, but practical research infrastructure for genomics.

My earlier work on chromatin architecture and 3D genomics continues to inform this agenda by grounding sequence-based modeling in biological mechanism.
